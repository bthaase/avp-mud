Design Documentation: ShockBOT Engine

The ShockBOT Engine creates, manages, and maintains the free-roaming Bots on AvP: Legends. The Bots are used as backup players when the game teams become unbalanced. The ShockBOT Engine places bots on the undermanned teams to try and even the playing field somewhat.

Potential Features:
 * Delayed Reaction Time, to simulate human delay, lag and error.
 * Goal-oriented Mission Tracking System, so they can complete missions. (Maybe)
 * Bot Personalities - Determines a bots actions, strategies and choices.
 * Flex loading. Bot 'pfiles' are saved to /bots, and loaded when needed.
 * Linked-list tracking for multiple bots.
 * Smart Equipping. Mixes Personality settings with weapon stats when getting gear.
 * XP Gaining. Bots gain XP like players, and use it to shop and level.
 * Balanced Load. Loads bots with the average level online, for fairness.
 * Channel Support. Bots can communicate their actions with their side.
 * Selective Distribution. Bots are split evenly amongst the most active areas.
 * Shifting Personalities, so bots learn from their mistakes.

Technical Design: Making 'em a reality.

Several things must be considered when coding the bots. One of the major setbacks is the current configuration with the mobiles. Namely, the fact that NONE of the mobiles have PCDATA structures, so they dont have skill lists or anything. The easiest way to solve this would be to give every bot a PCDATA structure when its created, and flag it similar to a player. In theory, the only problem then should be descriptors... or the lack thereof. Bots do not have descriptors, as they have no connection. So that leaves two options for that: 

 * Code a kind of 'Null' descriptor that dumps everything into oblivion.
 * Code better support for null descriptors, so I can just leave it blank.

Once thats taken care of, it becomes a fairly simple matter of teaching them how to act. So far, it seemed best to do this through state-based logic. Each Bot has states that indicator his or her current goals, objectives and other important factors. Then, I can interweave state-change commands into the existing functions - For instance, when a bot is 'shot', we want to change his state to one of defense, or possibly aggressiveness - If he's a freaking pyscho. We can also have diffrent reactions based on their combat preferences and the kinds of triggers. For instance:

 Bob is a marine bot. His favorite weapon is a shotgun, and he happens to be using it right now. Now, Bob gets clawed at by an alien, who happens to be in the same room. *BLAM* - Bob's state changed to one of aggressive assault, and vaporized the poor little bastard. Now, Say Bob is shot at from a distance, by a snipe. Well, a shotgun isn't exactly a ranged weapon, so Bob's state changes to 'evasive' and he gets the hell out of their. After he stops running, Bob is hit with a spray from a nearby room. Likely it wasn't targetted for Bob, but you never know. Bob's state changes to cover instead of evasion, and he goes one room in a direction to get outside the line of fire. Bob is good bot.
